--- title: Blue Balls Inc. - Day 9 layout: post category: sagamedevvii-blueballs ---

Another major shift in the idea of the game. Originally I was going to make something tile based (as I mentioned, based on blue ball machines). But then after playing around with it for a while, I realised that this mechanic isn't very fun. It's a shame because the random level generater was quite fun to work on. Never mind, on to the next idea!

The game is to be a physics based puzzle game where the goal is to get the ball from one side of the room to the other, by building machines to get it there.

Obviously through early versions of the game the machines are going to be simple, about balance, etc. But if I have time before the end of the month I hope to allow for some slightly more crazy machines which rely on the wire being cut which drops a hammer which causes the ruler to fall off the table and send the ball flying towards the exit... or whatever.

I didn't get far yesterday apart from reading about Box2D but today I made some progress. Right now I have the ball starting in a position, and the player can start and reset the scene (turning the physics on and off) - in the final version I expect that everything except for the ball will be moving in the building mode, and everything will be moving in the "playing" mode.

Some good progress today. Tomorrow I hope to get some basic interaction going on. As always, you can see the latest version at blueballs.jscott.me.
